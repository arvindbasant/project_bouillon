## 2. Exec summary *(one page, insight-led — this is what Wez reads)*

**North America Bouillon — Current-State Headlines**

- **5,246,057 cases produced across 3 sites in 2025.** Lerma 3.84M (73%), Indy 819K (16%), Batavia 585K (11%).
- **The Lerma → Indy shift is already in motion.** 44% of all 2025 cases sit in SKUs running at two sites; the monthly pattern for the nine Lerma ↔ Indy SKUs shows a clear mid-year handoff (e.g. `KNR MEX BOUIL CHK 6 2 LB` — 1.18M cases — runs heavy at Lerma Jan–Oct and ramps sharply at Indy from October).
- **Indy today is a single-line, two-format, Retail-only site.** One line ("15"), two pack sizes (2 lb + 15.9 oz), 100% US Retail, 0 UFS cases. Everything else in the network runs somewhere Indy does not.
- **Scenario 1 (Maximize Indy) is addressable against ~1.5M Lerma cases** — specifically the 2 lb Large Granulated and 15.9 oz Small Granulated that Indy already runs. Gated on **line-15 capacity headroom + possible second-line decision**. Data not yet available.
- **Scenario 2 (Full consolidation) is a capability build, not a transfer.** ~2.9M cases (56% of the network) sit in **formats and technologies Indy has never run.** Biggest pieces: Lerma's 7.9 oz Small Granulated (1.79M), Batavia's 4.4 lb Caldo UFS (457K), Lerma's 7.9 lb UFS (372K). **The UFS footprint at Indy has to be built from zero.**
- **Fragilities flagged:** Indy = single line (no redundancy); Batavia technology monopoly (Caldo / MixMod / Natural have no second source); ~3.8M cases of cross-border flow from Lerma to US customers (duty, customs, FX); in-flight transfer status makes the "current state" a moving target.
- **What's missing:** cost per case, gross margin, Indy/Lerma/Batavia line capacity, named co-mans, R&D harmonization roadmap status. Formal data request list on the call.
